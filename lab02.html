AI Tool Evaluation

Introduction

For this lab, I explored NotebookLM and Leonardo.AI to understand how specialized AI can assist in research synthesis and visual communication within the DCDA field.

Tool 1: NotebookLM

Capabilities

NotebookLM excels at "source-grounded" intelligence. It generates summaries and answers based strictly on uploaded documents, which significantly reduces hallucinations. However, it struggles with creative tasks outside the provided context and cannot interpret complex data visualization within PDFs very well.

Appropriate Use

It is perfect for synthesizing large amounts of course readings into study guides. It is a poor choice for general web searching or news, as its knowledge is limited to your specific "notebook" uploads.

Ethical Considerations

Google claims data uploaded isn't used to train their global models, which protects IP. However, relying on it risks creating an intellectual echo chamber by ignoring perspectives not included in the uploaded files.

Tool 2: Leonardo.AI

Capabilities

Leonardo.AI produces high-fidelity visual assets with granular control over style and lighting. It is excellent for rapid prototyping but struggles with precise text rendering and maintaining anatomical accuracy in complex human poses.

Appropriate Use

A good use case is generating concept art or UI backgrounds for digital projects. A poor use case is attempting to create photorealistic "news" images, which risks spreading misinformation.

Ethical Considerations

The tool is trained on vast datasets often without explicit artist consent. While they offer "commercially safe" models, the question of attribution for the original artists in the training set remains a major concern.

Broader Reflections

In a DCDA context, AI shifts the focus from "production" to "curation and auditing." While AI can generate the baseline data or images, the human role is to ensure ethical accuracy and cultural relevance. When evaluating future tools, I will prioritize transparency in training data and the ability to verify outputs against primary sources.
